Oliver Twist

Author: Oliver Twist

"Oliver Twist" is a social commentary novel by Charles Dickens that tells the story of an orphan named Oliver who is born in a workhouse and endures a childhood of poverty and hardship. He escapes to London and falls in with a gang of young thieves led by the Artful Dodger and the criminal Fagin. The novel vividly portrays the grim realities of poverty, crime, and social injustice in 19th-century London. Through Oliver's experiences , Dickens critiques the Poor Laws of the time and explores themes of good versus evil, the search for identity, and the possibility of redemption.
