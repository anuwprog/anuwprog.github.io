Little Dorrit

Author: Charles Dickens

"Little Dorrit" is a novel by Charles Dickens that satirizes the social and bureaucratic failings of Victorian England, particularly the Marshalsea debtor's prison. The story primarily follows Amy Dorrit, born and raised in the prison, and Arthur Clennam, who returns to England after years abroad to unravel a family mystery connected to the Dorrit family's misfortune. The novel explores themes of imprisonment, both literal and metaphorical, the corrupting influence of wealth and social status, and the power of love and compassion in a flawed society. It is a complex and insightful work that critiques the institutions and values of Dickens' time.
